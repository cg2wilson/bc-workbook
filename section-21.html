    Section 2.1 Measures of Change over an Interval

    Subsection 2.1.1 Formulas

    Let \(f\) be a function with input values \(x_1,x_2\) such that \(x_1 \lt x_2\text{.}\)

    Change.

    \(f(x_2) - f(x_1)\)

    Percent Change.

    \(\dfrac{f(x_2) - f(x_1)}{f(x_1)}\cdot 100\%\)

    Average Rate of Change (AROC).

    \(\dfrac{f(x_2) - f(x_1)}{x_2-x_1}\)
    When giving interpretations, we have four considerations:
    1. When is this event happening? Be sure to specify the interval.
    2. What is happening? Specify the quantity which is changing.
    3. How is it changing? Specify whether or not the quantity is increasing or decreasing.
    4. By how much is it changing? Include proper units.

    Example 2.1.1.

    If \(f\) denotes the number of students enrolled in Math 1743 and \(x\) is the number of academic years after the 2000-2001 academic year, interpret the expression \(f(10) = 1552\text{.}\)
    Solution.
    10 years after the 2000-2001 academic year, the number of students enrolled in Math 1743 was 1552 students

    Subsection 2.1.2 Examples

    Example 2.1.2.

    The average temperature in Norman during the last week of September is given in the table below:
    Time Temperature (\(^\circ\) F) Time Temperature (\(^\circ\) F)
    7am 49 1pm 80
    8am 58 2pm 80
    9am 66 3pm 78
    10am 72 4pm 74
    11am 76 5pm 69
    noon 79 6pm 62
    1. Give the average rate of change in temperature between 11am and 4pm. Write a sentence interpreting your result.
    2. Find the percent change in temperature between 9am and noon, and round your answer to the nearest hundredth. Write a sentence interpreting your result.
    Solution.
    1. AROC is \(-0.4^\circ\) F per hour. Between 11am and 4pm, the average temperature in Norman decreased by \(0.4^\circ\) F per hour
      For (a) and (b), you will want to stress the verb tense in the "how" statement. When we interpret instantaneous rates of change, the tense changes to present progressive.
    2. The percent change is 19.7%. Between 9am and 12pm, the average temperature in Norman increased by 19.7%.

    Example 2.1.3.

    Airtran posted a revenue of $603.7 million dollars in the second quarter of 2009 compared with revenue of $693.4 million during the second quarter of 2008. Write a sentence interpreting each of the following:
    1. Find the change in revenue between the second quarter of 2008 and the second quarter of 2009.
    2. Find the percent change between the second quarter of 2008 and second quarter end of 2009.
    3. Find the average rate of change between the second quarter of 2008 and the second quarter of 2009.
    Solution.
    1. Between the second quarter of 2008 and the second quarter of 2009, Airtran's revenue increased by 89.7 million dollars
    2. Between the second quarter of 2008 and the second quarter of 2009, Airtran's revenue increased by 14.86%.
    3. Between the second quarter of 2008 and the second quarter of 2009, Airtran's revenue increased by 89.7 million dollars per year.

    Example 2.1.4.

    The American Indian, Eskimo, and Aleut populations in the United States was 362 thousand in 1930, and 4.5 million in 2005. Write a sentence interpreting each of the following, and round to two decimals if necessary:
    1. Find the change in population between 1930 and 2005.
    2. Find the percent change between between 1930 and 2005.
    3. Find the average rate of change between 1930 and 2005.
    Solution.
    1. Between 1930 and 2005, the American Indian, Eskimo, and Aleut populations in the US increased by 4.14 million people.
      Be mindful that the unit in 1930 is "thousand people" not "million people", so you have to do some conversion
    2. Between 1930 and 2005, the American Indian, Eskimo, and Aleut populations in the US increased by 1143.65%.
    3. Between 1930 and 2005, the American Indian, Eskimo, and Aleut populations in the US increased by 0.06 million people per year.

    Example 2.1.5.

    OU Parking Services commissioned a projection of its profit (in thousands of dollars) when commuter parking passes are set certain prices.
    Price (dollars) 200 250 300 350 400 450
    Profit (thousand dollars) 2080 2520 2760 2820 2700 2380
    1. Find a model for the data.
    2. Calculate the average rate of change of profit when the parking pass price rises from $200 to $350.
    3. Calculate the average rate of change of profit when the parking pass price rises from $350 to $450.
    4. Calculate the percent change for parts (b) and (c).
    Solution.
    1. \(P(x) = -0.037x^2 + 25.529x-1527.14\) thousand dollars projected profit for OU parking when commuter parking passes are \(x\) dollars.
    2. 4.93 thousand dollars profit per dollar
    3. -4.4 thousand dollars profit per dollar
    4. In (b), it's 35.58%; in (c), it's -15.60%.

    Example 2.1.6.

    The CDC modeled the number of Zika cases diagnosed in Brazil between January and July of 2016 with the formula
    \begin{equation*} z(t) = 2.75(1.04^t)\text{ thousand cases} \end{equation*}
    where \(t\) is the number of months since January 2016.
    1. Calculate and write a sentence of interpretation for the average rate of change in the number of Brazilians diagnosed with Zika between January 2016 and July 2016.
    2. Calculate the percentage change in part (a).
    Solution.
    1. Between January 2016 and July 2016, the number of Zika cases diagnosed in Brazil increased by 0.12 thousand cases per month.
    2. 26.53%

    Example 2.1.7.

    The function \(c(t)\) represents the number of students in line at Chick-Fil-A, \(t\) hours after 11:00am, and \(q(t)\) represents the number of students in line at Quizno's, \(t\) hours after 11:00am. Write a sentence interpreting the following expressions.
    1. \(\displaystyle c(3) = 15\)
    2. \(\displaystyle q(1) = 8\)
    3. \(\displaystyle (c+q)(0) = 12\)
    Solution.
    1. 3 hours after 11am, the number of students in the Chick-Fil-A line was 15.
    2. 1 hour after 11am, the number of students in the Quizno's line was 8.
    3. At 11am, the number of students in the Chick-Fil-A and Quizno's lines was 12.
