    Section 4.3 Absolute Extreme Points

    Definition 4.3.1. Absolute Extrema.

    A function \(f\) has an absolute maximum at input \(c\) if \(f(c)\) is greater than or equal to all other outputs. Similarly, \(f\) has an absolute minmium at \(c\) if \(f(c)\) is less than or equal to all other outputs.
    In practice, there is very little distinction between what we did in 4.2 and what we do here in 4.3; the key difference is determining whether or not the particular max/min is the greatest or the least output value.

    Subsection 4.3.1 Examples

    Example 4.3.2.

    Consider the function \(f(x) = 6x^4-6x^3-5x^2+5x-1\)
    1. Locate any extreme values of the function on the interval \(-2\leq x\leq 2\)
    2. Classify the extreme values you found in part (a)
    Solution.
    In this question (and in this section), students will confuse the procedure in Section 4.2 with the procedure in this section. Be very clear about setting the input interval/viewing window appropriately in the calculator.
    I'm also not a fan of the phrasing "classify the extreme values" that currently exists in the workbook, since you aren't actually doing that. I would say try to make the process clear independent of my bad phrasing.
    1. The critical numbers are \(x=-0.58,0.38,0.96\)
    2. The absolute max is \((-2,113)\) and the absolute min is \((-0.58,-3.73)\)

    Example 4.3.3.

    Consider the function \(g(t) = -0.37t^3 + 5.34t^2 -9.66t + 96.93\)
    1. Locate any extreme values of the function on the interval \(0\leq x\leq 11\text{.}\)
    2. Classify the extreme values you found in part (a)
    Solution.
    1. The critical points are \((1.01,92.24)\) and \((8.61,173.46)\)
    2. The absolute max is at \((8.61,173.46)\) and the absolute min is at \((1.01,92.24)\)

    Example 4.3.4.

    Consider the function \(h(p) = (e^{2-p})(3^p-p^2)\)
    1. Locate any extreme values of the function on the interval \(-1\leq p\leq 4\text{.}\)
    2. Classify the extreme values you found in part (a)
    Solution.
    1. The critical points are at \((0.05,7.41)\) and \((1.64,4.83)\)
    2. The absolute min is at \((-1,-13.39)\) and the absolute max is at \((4,8.8)\)

    Example 4.3.5.

    Consider the function \(y(x) = 0.75x^4-3.86x^2+10.18x + 22.186\)
    1. Locate any extreme values of the function on the interval \((-\infty,\infty)\)
    2. Classify the extreme values you found in part (a)
    Solution.
    The interval will be weird for them because they're not given a closed interval (hence you can't use the Extreme Value Theorem, like the other questions sort of expect). Remind the students that they know the shape/end behavior of the function because it's a quartic with positive leading coefficient.
    The window may be hard to figure, but starting with \([-10,10]\) is a good starting point.
    1. The only critical number is at \((-2.06,-1.66)\)
    2. The absolute minimum is at \((-2.06,-1.66)\text{;}\) there is no absolute maximum.

    Example 4.3.6.

    Find and classify the absolute and relative maxima/minima for the function \(f(x) = 3x^4-16x^3+18x^2\) on \([-1,4]\text{.}\) If necessary, round to the nearest hundredth.
    Solution.
    The absolute max is at \((-1,37)\text{,}\) the absolute min is at \((3,-27)\text{.}\) \((0,0)\) and \((3,-27)\) are relative mins, and \((1,5)\) is a relative max.

    Example 4.3.7.

    Find the absolute and relative extrema for the function \(f(x) = x^3-3x^2 + 1\) on \(-\dfrac{1}{2}\leq x\leq 4\text{.}\) If necessary, round to the nearest hundredth.
    Solution.
    \((0,1)\) is a relative max, \((2,-3)\) is an absolute and local min, and \((4,17)\) is an absolute max

    Example 4.3.8.

    Find and classify all extrema of the function \(f(x) = 12 + 4x - x^2\) on \([0,5]\text{.}\) If necessary, round to the nearest hundredth.
    Solution.
    The absolute and local max is at \((2,14)\text{,}\) with an absolute min at \((5,7)\)

    Example 4.3.9.

    Find and classify all extrema of the function \(f(t) = (t^2-4)^3\) on \([-2,3]\text{.}\) If necessary, round to the nearest hundredth.
    Solution.
    \((0,-64)\) is an absolute/local min, \((3,125)\) is the absolute max

    Example 4.3.10.

    Find the relative and absolute maxima and minima of the function \(g(x) = \dfrac{x}{x^2-x+1}\) on \([0,3]\text{.}\) If necessary, round to the nearest hundredth.
    Solution.
    The absolute min is at \((0,0)\) and the absolute/local max is at \((1,1)\)

    Example 4.3.11.

    The sales of a new Starbucks drink are approximated by the function \(S(x) = -.002 x^4 + .093 x^3 - 1.38 x^2 + 6.573 x + 5.393\) thousand dollars, \(x\) months after its introduction. Round your answers to the nearest hundredth.
    1. The absolute maximum of drink sales between month 1 and month 15 was BLANK and occurred BLANK months after release.
    2. The absolute minimum of drink sales between month 1 and month 15 was BLANK and occurred BLANK months after release.
    3. Calculate the percent rate of change 4 months after introduction, to the nearest hundredth as a percent.
    Solution.
    The exam often has questions phrased like this; ensuring that they are used to the phrasing should help them when it comes to the exam. Be sure to emphasize the rounding AND the units on the answers.
    1. The absolute maximum of drink sales between month 1 and month 15 was 15.18 thousand dollars and occurred 3.49 months after release.
    2. The absolute minimum of drink sales between month 1 and month 15 was 4.74 thousand dollars and occurred 12.41 months after release.
    3. -3.46% per month

    Example 4.3.12.

    The quantity of a drug in the bloodstream \(t\) hours after a tablet is swallowed is given by \(q(t) = 20(e^{-t}-e^{-3t})\) \(\mu g\text{.}\)
    1. How much of the drug is in the bloodstream at time \(t = 0\text{?}\)
    2. Over the first twelve hours, at what time is the amount of drug in the bloodstream at its highest? What is the maximum amount?
    Solution.
    1. 0 \(\mu g\)
    2. The max occurs at \((0.55\text{ hours},7.70\text{ }\mu g)\)
