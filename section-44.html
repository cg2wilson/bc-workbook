    Section 4.4 Inflection Points & Second Derivatives

    Subsection 4.4.1 Definitions

    Definition 4.4.1. Inflection Point.

    An inflection point is the point on the graph of a function where the function changes concavity
    An inflection point gives the point of most/least rapid change of the function.

    Theorem 4.4.2. Properties of the Second Derivative.

    Let \(f(x)\) be a twice-differentiable function defined on input interval \([a,b]\) with \(a \lt c \lt b\)
    • \(f\) has an inflection point at \(x=c\) if and only if \(f''(c) = 0\) and changes sign
    • If \(f''(c)\) is positive, then \(f\) is concave up
    • If \(f''(c)\) is positive, then \(f'\) is increasing
    • If \(f''(c)\) is negative, then \(f\) is concave down
    • If \(f''(c)\) is negative, tthen \(f'\) is decreasing

    Theorem 4.4.3. Second Derivative Test.

    Let \(f(x)\) be a twice-differentiable function defined on input interval \([a,b]\) with \(a\lt c\lt b\)
    • If \(f'(c) = 0\) and \(f''(c)\gt 0\text{,}\) then \(f\) has a relative minimum at \(c\text{.}\)
    • If \(f'(c) = 0\) and \(f''(c)\lt 0\text{,}\) then \(f\) has a relative maximum at \(c\text{.}\)

    Subsection 4.4.2 Examples

    Example 4.4.4.

    The percentage of people living in California in 2007 who were born in the state can be modeled as
    \begin{equation*} P(x) = -0.0016x^3 + 0.224x^2-10.577x + 204.8\text{ percent} \end{equation*}
    where \(x\) is the age of the resident.
    1. Find the inflection point of the function \(P\text{.}\)
    2. Give a sentence of interpretation for the age between 20 and 70 at which the percentage of California residents who were born in the state was decreasing least rapidly.
    Solution.
    There are several ways of identifying inflection points on a calculator. My opinion is that the easiest (for them) thing to do is to graph the derivative and identify maxes/mins on the derivative graph rather than graphing the second derivative.
    Whichever method you choose, I suggest showing them one and having them stick with it, in order to minimize confusion between the processes they have to learn in Chapter 4.
    1. \(\displaystyle (46.67,36.42)\)
    2. The percentage of people living in California was decreasing least rapidly for those aged 46.67 years old and was decreasing by 0.128% per year

    Example 4.4.5.

    For the function
    \begin{equation*} f(t)=-2.1t^2 + 7t \end{equation*}
    1. Write the first and second derivative
    2. Identify any inflection points, and label them as the point of least rapid or most rapid change.
    Solution.
    1. \(f'(t) = -4.2t + 7\) and \(f''(t) = -4.2\)
    2. There are no inflection points

    Example 4.4.6.

    The percentage of new material that an average college student will retain after studying for \(t\) hours without a break can be modeled as
    \begin{equation*} p(t) = \dfrac{83}{1+5.94e^{-0.969t}}\text{ percent} \end{equation*}
    1. Find when the retention rate is increasing most rapidly.
    2. Determine the rate of change of retention as well as the percentage of retention at the input found in item (a).
    3. Describe the difference between the direction of \(p\) and \(p'\) to the right of the input found in item (a).
    4. Explain what happens to the student's retention rate after the input found in item (a)
    Solution.
    1. \(t=1.84\) hours
    2. \(p'(1.84) = 20.11\) % per hour, and \(p(1.84) = 41.50%\)
    3. \(p\) is increasing, but \(p'\) starts to decrease
    4. After 1.84 hours of studying, the retention percentage is increasing at a decreasing rate

    Example 4.4.7.

    For the function
    \begin{equation*} g(s) = 32s^3 + 2.1s^2 + 7s \end{equation*}
    1. Write the first and second derivative.
    2. Identify any inflection points, and label them as the point of least rapid or most rapid change.
    Solution.
    1. \(g'(s) = 96s^2+4.2s+7\) and \(g''(s) = 192s+4.2\)
    2. \((-0.02,-0.15)\) is a point of least rapid change.

    Example 4.4.8.

    For the function
    \begin{equation*} h(x) = e^{3x} - \ln 3x \end{equation*}
    1. Write the first and second derivative.
    2. Identify any inflection points, and label them as the point of least rapid or most rapid change.
    Solution.
    This problem can probably be skipped.
    1. \(h'(x) = 3e^{3x} - \dfrac{1}{x}\) and \(h''(x) = 9e^{3x} + \dfrac{1}{x^2}\)
    2. There are none

    Example 4.4.9.

    For the function
    \begin{equation*} k(t) = \dfrac{16}{1+2.1e^{3.9t}} \end{equation*}
    1. Write the first and second derivative.
    2. Identify any inflection points, and label them as the point of least rapid or most rapid change.
    Solution.
    The difficulty in this problem is in finding the second derivative of a logistic. They will probably never have to do it on a test, but it's good (I think) to make them practice all the rules together in service of some goal.
    1. \(k'(t) = -16(1+2.1e^{3.9t})^{-2}(2.1e^{3.9t}\cdot 3.9)\) and \(k''(t) = (32(1+2.1e^{3.9t})^{-3})(2.1e^{3.9t}\cdot 3.9)(2.1e^{3.9t}\cdot 3.9) + (-16(1+2.1e^{3.9t})^{-2})(2.1e^{3.9t}\cdot 3.9\cdot 3.9)\)
    2. \((0.19,8)\) is a point of most rapid change

    Example 4.4.10.

    For the function
    \begin{equation*} f(x) = -x^3+12x^2 + 36x + 45 \end{equation*}
    1. Write the first and second derivative.
    2. Identify any inflection points, and label them as the point of least rapid or most rapid change.
    Solution.
    1. \(f'(x) = -3x^2+24x+36\) and \(f''(x) = -6x + 24\)
    2. \((4,317)\) is a point of most rapid change

    Example 4.4.11.

    The table below shows the monthly revenue levels associated with various monthly levels of advertising by a furniture store.
    Advertising (in hundreds of dollars) 1 4 7 10 13 16 19
    Revenue (in thousands of dollars) 114 210 265 299 338 449 632
    1. Find the complete cubic model \(R(x)\) for the data (do not align the input).
    2. Write the complete rate of change model for \(R(x)\text{.}\)
    3. Find \(R''(x)\text{.}\)
    4. Find the inflection point of \(R(x)\) on the interval \([0,19]\text{.}\) Round both coordinates to the hundredths place and be sure to label them with units.
    5. Find the rate of change at the inflection point. Round to the hundredths place and include units in your answer.
    Solution.
    1. \(R(x) = 0.212x^3-5.399x^2+56.108x + 61.936\) thousand dollars revenue for a furniture store when \(x\) hundred dollars are spent on advertising
    2. \(R'(x) = 0.626x^2-10.798x+56.108\) thousand dollars (revenue) per hundred dollars (advertising), rate of change of revenue for a furniture store when \(x\) hundred dollars are spent on advertising
    3. \(\displaystyle R''(x) = 1.272x-10.798\)
    4. \(x = 8.49\) hundred dollars and \(R(8.49) = 278.82\) thousand dollars
    5. \(R'(8.49) = 10.26\) thousand dollars per hundred dollars
