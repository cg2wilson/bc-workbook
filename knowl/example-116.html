Example 2.5.5.

The time it takes an average athlete to swim 100 meters freestyle at age \(x\) years can be modeled as
\begin{equation*} t(x) = 0.181x^2 - 8.463x + 147.376\text{ seconds} \end{equation*}
  1. Calculate the swim time at age 13 to the nearest second.
  2. Use the algebraic method to develop a formula for the derivative of \(t\) (ie, find \(t'(x)\)).
  3. How quickly is the time to swim 100 meters freestyle changing for an average 13-year-old athlete? Round to the nearest hundredth and interpret the result.
  4. Compute the percent rate of change of swimmers' time at age 13, to the nearest tenth.
