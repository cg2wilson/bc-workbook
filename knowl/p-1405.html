Alternately, for a quotient function, we may employ the quotient rule. If \(k(x) = \dfrac{f(x)}{g(x)}\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} k'(x) = \dfrac{f'(x)g(x) - f(x)g'(x)}{[g(x)]^2} \end{equation*}
This can be remembered as
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{\text{low}\cdot d\text{(high)} - \text{high }\cdot d\text{(low)} }{\text{(low)}^2} \end{equation*}
