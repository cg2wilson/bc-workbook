We have two formulas for discretely compounding interest:
\begin{align*} I \amp = \dfrac{r}{n}\\ F_d(t) \amp = P\lrpar{1 + \dfrac{r}{n}}^{nt} \end{align*}
where \(P\) is the principal, \(r\) is the rate (as a decimal), \(t\) is the time (in years), and \(n\) is the number of compounds (in a years)
