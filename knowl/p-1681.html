Step 3: In order to write the model with a single input variable, we need a second equation to eliminate one of the variables. We know that \(V = 10lw\text{.}\) But, we are also told that the maximum linear measurement is given by \(l + w + h = 45\text{.}\) Since \(h = 10\text{,}\) this becomes \(l + w + 10 = 45\text{,}\) so \(l + w = 35\text{.}\) Now we can solve for either \(l\) or \(w\text{.}\) Choose to solve for length. Thus, \(l = 35 - w\text{.}\) We can substitute this into the equation for volume:
\begin{align*} V \amp 10lw\\ \amp 10(35-w)w \\ \amp -10w^2+350w \end{align*}
This gives us an equation in terms of a single variable, one which we can optimize.
