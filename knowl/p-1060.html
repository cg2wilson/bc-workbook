Let \(a\) be fixed, and let \(x\) be some point on \(f\) other than \(a\text{.}\) Then, the slope of the secant line is given by
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{f(x)-f(a)}{x-a} \end{equation*}
Taking limits, we have the definition of the derivative of \(f\) at a point \(a\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} f'(a) = \ds \lim_{x\to a} \dfrac{f(x)-f(a)}{x-a} \end{equation*}
