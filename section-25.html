    Section 2.5 The Derivative, Algebraically

    Subsection 2.5.1 The Derivative, Algebraically

    Definition 2.5.1. Derivative (Algebraic Definition).

    Let \(f(x)\) be a function defined on the open interval \((a,b)\text{,}\) and \(x\in (a,b)\text{.}\) Then, the derivative of \(f\) at point \(x\) is given by the formula
    \begin{equation*} \ds \lim_{h\to 0}\dfrac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h} \end{equation*}

    Question 2.5.2.

    Why is this definition the same as the one in Section 2.4?
    Solution.
    Answers vary
    At this point, I take time to draw some pictures to show what the earlier definition of the derivative "meant" and what the new one "means".
    It is useful to remember a few things from algebra when doing these calculations:
    • \(\displaystyle \sqrt{x + y}\neq \sqrt{x} + \sqrt{y}\)
    • \(\displaystyle (a+b)^2 \neq a^2 + b^2\)
    • \(\displaystyle \dfrac{a}{b+c}\neq \dfrac{a}{b} + \dfrac{a}{c}\)
    When we algebraically find the derivative of a function, there is a four-step process which makes the algebra much simpler, and the derivative easier to find. The steps are:
    1. Find and simplify \(f(x+h)\)
    2. Find and simplify \(f(x+h)-f(x)\)
    3. Find and simplify \(\dfrac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}\)
    4. Compute \(\ds \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}\)
    This is demonstrated below.

    Example 2.5.3.

    Algebraically find the derivative of the function \(f(x) = x^2 \) using the four-step process.
    Solution.
    \(f(x) = 2x\)
    Algebra tends not to be a strength for many students. Go deliberately and carefully; handling the notation of \(f(x+h)\) is a common tripping point.

    Example 2.5.4.

    Algebraically find the derivative of the function \(f(x) = 5x -2\) using the four-step process.
    Solution.
    \(f'(x) =5\)

    Example 2.5.5.

    The time it takes an average athlete to swim 100 meters freestyle at age \(x\) years can be modeled as
    \begin{equation*} t(x) = 0.181x^2 - 8.463x + 147.376\text{ seconds} \end{equation*}
    1. Calculate the swim time at age 13 to the nearest second.
    2. Use the algebraic method to develop a formula for the derivative of \(t\) (ie, find \(t'(x)\)).
    3. How quickly is the time to swim 100 meters freestyle changing for an average 13-year-old athlete? Round to the nearest hundredth and interpret the result.
    4. Compute the percent rate of change of swimmers' time at age 13, to the nearest tenth.
    Solution.
    1. \(t(13) = 68\) seconds
    2. \(\displaystyle t'(x) = 0.362x-8.463\)
    3. \(t'(13) = -3.76\) seconds per year. At age 13, the average athlete's 100m freestyle swim time is decreasing by 3.76 seconds per year
    4. -5.5% per year

    Example 2.5.6.

    Algebraically determine the derivative of \(f(t) = \dfrac{1}{2}t^2 -\dfrac{1}{3}\text{,}\) and evaluate \(\dfrac{df}{dt}\bigg|_{t=1}\)
    Solution.
    \(f'(1) = 1\)

    Example 2.5.7.

    Algebraically determine the derivative of \(k(r) = r^2-2r^3\text{,}\) and evaluate \(k'(0)\)
    Solution.
    I usually leave this one as practice, explicitly because there's a lot of algebra that they have to navigate, but I do give the answers so that they can verify their work.
    \(k'(r) = 2r-6r^2\text{;}\) \(k'(0) = 0\)
